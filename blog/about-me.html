About Me

2025-08-31

I am a pre-doctoral research professional at the Fama-Miller Center for Research in Finance at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business. My work focuses on the micro-foundations of power within political economy, the dynamics of macro-finance, and the nuances of behavioral economics, with a supplementary interest in systems engineering. I am driven by a fascination with using mathematical and statistical tools to untangle the threads of causality woven into our world.

Before joining Booth, I earned my bachelor's degree from Tsinghua University. Prior to that, I spent a memorable time at The High School Affiliated to Renmin University of China (RDFZ).

Outside of my academic work, I'm a fan of cinematic games, like The Last of Us, and JRPGs such as the Persona series. I also enjoy a wide range of anime, from classics like Ghost in the Shell, Psycho-Pass, Steins;Gate, and Puella Magi Madoka Magica to contemporary hits like BanG Dream! It's MyGO!!!!!. My interests also include Japanese literature and science fiction.

I currently reside in Hyde Park, Chicago. Before this, I spent the first 22 years of my life in Beijing, particularly in the Haidian District.
