I am a pre-doctoral research professional at the University of Chicago Booth School of Business, where I work with Professors Leland Bybee, Suproteem Sarkar, and Amir Sufi. I hold a bachelor's degree from Tsinghua University. My research interests lie at the intersection of political economy and finance, with a particular focus on leveraging computational methods from computer science to address open questions in economics and finance.

Software

Wayback4AI

An open-source Python toolkit for large-scale historical website data collection from the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine. Designed for researchers who need longitudinal web text panels, the tool interfaces with the CDX API for metadata retrieval and supports batch downloading of historical snapshots.

StringMatcher

A high-performance Python toolkit for large-scale fuzzy string matching using MinHash LSH and multiple similarity metrics. Combines fast candidate pair generation with RapidFuzz scoring, making it ideal for entity resolution, record linkage, and data deduplication across millions of records.
